# 06 — Timeline: Life, Career, and Publishing

> Chronological timeline of Ray Dalio's life from birth to present day. Organized by era.

---

## Early Life: 1949–1971

| Year | Age | Event | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|-------|
| Aug 8, 1949 | 0 | **Born** Raymond Thomas Dalio in Jackson Heights, Queens, NYC | Son of Italian-American jazz musician Marino Dallolio and homemaker Ann |
| 1957 | 8 | Family moves to Manhasset, Long Island | Middle-class neighborhood |
| 1961 | 12 | **Buys first stock**: Northeast Airlines | Triples his money — hooking him on investing for life |
| 1961 | 12 | Starts caddying at a golf course | Overhears Wall Street conversations from members |
| 1967 | 18 | Graduates Herricks High School | — |
| 1971 | 22 | **BS in Finance** from C.W. Post College (LIU) | — |
| 1971 | 22 | Works as a clerk on the **New York Stock Exchange** floor | First exposure to professional trading |

## Education and Early Career: 1971–1975

| Year | Age | Event | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|-------|
| 1971-1972 | 21-23 | Works as a clerk on NYSE floor | Experiences the collapse of the Bretton Woods system firsthand |
| 1973 | 24 | **MBA from Harvard Business School** | — |
| 1973 | 24 | Trades commodities from a converted barn in Wilton, CT | Started while still at HBS |
| 1973 | 24 | Director of Commodities at **Dominick & Dominick LLC** | Brief stint |
| 1974 | 25 | Futures trader/broker at **Shearson Hayden Stone** | Works under Sandy Weill (later Citigroup CEO) |
| Dec 31, 1974 | 25 | **Fired** from Shearson Hayden Stone after punching his boss at a company New Year's Eve party | "I was drunk and I made a terrible mistake" — career turning point |

## Bridgewater Founding and Early Years: 1975–1990

| Year | Age | Event | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|-------|
| 1975 | 26 | **Founds Bridgewater Associates** from his two-bedroom NYC apartment | Starts as a wealth advisory firm serving corporate clients |
| 1975-1980s | — | Bridgewater publishes "Daily Observations" — paid subscription research report | Analyzes global market trends; builds Bridgewater's reputation |
| 1981 | 32 | Opens Bridgewater office in Westport, Connecticut | Moves to be closer to where he and Barbara wanted to raise their family |
| 1982 | 33 | **NEAR-BANKRUPTCY**: Wrong bet on the economy | The most important failure of his career |
| 1982 | 33 | Lays off all employees | — |
| 1982 | 33 | Borrows $4,000 from father for rent | Lowest point |
| 1982 | 33 | Sells second car to pay bills | — |
| 1985 | 36 | **McDonald's becomes a client** — Bridgewater's first major institutional client | "This was the break I needed" |
| 1987 | 38 | **Profits from the 1987 stock market crash** | First major public recognition of Dalio's macro ability |
| Late 1980s | — | World Bank pension fund and Eastman Kodak pension fund become clients | Major institutional credibility |

## Bridgewater Ascendancy: 1991–2010

| Year | Age | Event | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|-------|
| 1991 | 42 | **Launches Pure Alpha** — Bridgewater's flagship hedge fund strategy | Systematized macro investing using multiple uncorrelated return streams |
| 1996 | 47 | **Launches All Weather** — pioneers risk parity approach | Portfolio that balances risk across economic environments |
| 2000 | 51 | Bridgewater continues steady growth, adding major institutional clients | — |
| 2003 | 54 | **Creates Dalio Foundation** (later Dalio Philanthropies) | — |
| 2005 | 56 | **Bridgewater becomes the world's largest hedge fund** | — |
| 2007 | 58 | Publishes "How the Economic Machine Works: A Template for Understanding What is Happening Now" | Correctly predicts the coming financial crisis |
| 2007 | 58 | Bridgewater **predicts the 2008 financial crisis** | Profits while most of Wall Street collapses |
| 2008 | 59 | Bridgewater's Pure Alpha fund has a strongly positive year while most hedge funds lose money | Cements Dalio's reputation |
