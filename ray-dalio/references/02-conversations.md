### 3. World Governments Summit, Dubai — "On the Brink of Capital War" (Feb 3, 2026)

**Format:** Live Speech + CNBC Coverage

**Link:** [CNBC Article](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/03/ray-dalio-warns-capital-war-gold-hedge.html)

**Summary:**
Major speech in Dubai where Dalio warns the world is "on the brink" of a capital war. This is one of his most direct warnings ever, marking a significant escalation in his public commentary from educational to urgently warning.

**Key Arguments:**
1. **Capital is being weaponized** — through trade embargoes, blocking access to capital markets, and using debt ownership as leverage
2. **The Greenland issue** — Trump's push for Greenland has created real fears among European holders of US-denominated assets
3. **US-Europe capital dependence** — European investors accounted for 80% of foreign purchases of US Treasuries between April and November 2025
4. **Historical parallel** — In the run-up to WWII, the US imposed sanctions on Japan; a similar dynamic could play out between the US and China

**On Gold:**
- "Gold is up about 65% from a year ago, and down about 16% from its high"
- "Perhaps central banks or sovereign wealth funds should say, what percentage of my portfolio should I have in gold?"
- Gold is a "very effective diversifier" — when bad times come, it does uniquely well

**Key Quote:** "We are on the brink. That means not in, but it means we are quite close to [capital war], and it would be very easy to go over the brink."

### 4. Fortune Op-Ed — "I've studied 500 years of history and fear we're entering the most dangerous phase" (Mar 14, 2026)

**Format:** Byline Op-Ed

**Link:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/14/ray-dalio-big-cycle-debt-crisis-political-disorder-world-order/)

**Summary:**
A landmark essay that serves as Dalio's clearest statement yet of where he believes we are in the Big Cycle. He explicitly declares we are now in **Stage 5**, which immediately precedes the breakdown (Stage 6).

**The Stage 5 Argument:**
Dalio identifies three markers present today that define Stage 5:

1. **Large and rapidly rising government debts** leading to concerns about the value of money, driving a movement out of fiat currencies and into gold
2. **Large income, wealth, and values gaps** leading to populism (MAGA and WOKE in the US) and "irreconcilable differences that can't be resolved with compromises and rule of law"
3. **Movement from multilateralism to unilateralism** — the breakdown of post-1945 international orders (NATO, WTO, etc.) and the rise of "gunboat diplomacy"

**Historical Context:**
- "In the 1930s, four major democracies (Germany, Japan, Italy, and Spain) became autocracies"
- "Plato wrote about this dynamic in The Republic in 375 BC"
- "The last major Stage 6 period began in 1929 and ended in 1945"

**The Only Hope:**
"Nothing is predestined. There is some chance our leaders individually and collectively will not fight and will draw people together to do the difficult, smart things necessary to handle these challenges well enough to beat the odds. Human nature being what it is, I'm not optimistic."

**Key Quote:** "It is indisputably clear that what is happening now is more analogous to pre-1945 times than the post-1945 times that we have gotten used to."

### 5. Substack: "2025" — Annual Market Reflection (Jan 2026)

**Format:** Long-form Article (75 pages)

**Link:** [raydalio.substack.com/p/2025](https://raydalio.substack.com/p/2025)

**Summary:**
Dalio's most comprehensive piece of market analysis published in 2026. A complete dissection of 2025 through the Big Cycle lens. This is not a prediction piece — it's a systematic review of what actually happened and why.

**Key Data Points:**
- **Gold:** +65% in USD terms — best-performing major asset class
- **S&P 500:** +18% in dollars, but -28% in gold-money terms
- **Non-US equities outperformance:** European stocks +23% vs US, Chinese stocks +21%, UK stocks +19%, Japanese stocks +10%
- **Bonds:** 10-year Treasury returned 9% in dollars, -34% in gold terms
- **Emerging markets:** EM stocks returned 34% overall
- **Magnificent 7:** 22% earnings growth; other S&P 493 had 9% earnings growth
- **Long-term expected equity returns:** ~4.7% (sub-10th percentile)

**The Big Issue — Currency:**
- Dollar fell 0.3% vs yen, 4% vs renminbi, 12% vs euro, 13% vs Swiss franc, 39% vs gold
- "All fiat currencies fell" — the weakest fell the most, the strongest (hardest) fell the least

**Political Analysis:**
- Trump administration's "levered bet on capitalism" — stimulative fiscal, reduced regulation, tariffs
- Shift from free-market capitalism to government-directed capitalism
- "The 'haves' (top 10%) don't see inflation as a problem; the bottom 60% feel overwhelmed"
- Prediction: Republicans lose House in 2026 midterms, leading to a "messy 2027"
- "A big fight is brewing between the hard right, now led by President Trump, and the hard left"
- The Zohran Mamdani/Bernie Sanders/AOC coalition represents the "anti-billionaire, Democratic socialist movement"

**Market Mechanics:**
- S&P earnings up 12% in dollar terms — 57% from sales growth, 43% from margin improvement
- "Some significant portion of margin improvements was due to technology efficiencies"
- Fed cutting rates and easing credit availability — risk premia declining
- Venture capital, private equity, and real estate still having problems (illiquid markets)

**Key Quote:** "In summary, just about everything went up a lot in dollar terms because of the big fiscal and monetary reflationary policies and are now relatively expansive."

## 2025

### 6. The All-In Podcast — David Friedberg Interview (Jan 28, 2025)

**Host:** David Friedberg

**Format:** Podcast (76 min)

**Links:**
- [Full Transcript](https://singjupost.com/transcript-of-ray-dalios-interview-on-the-all-in-podcast/)
- [Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/ray-dalio-the-all-in-interview/id1502871393?i=1000686341107)
- [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/episode/6G51zeAOLp77EnuE8vyJNr)

**Summary:**
One of Dalio's most important interviews of 2025. David Friedberg provides a detailed setup of the US fiscal situation ($36.4T debt, 125% debt-to-GDP, $2T deficit), and Dalio provides his most specific explanation of the Big Debt Cycle mechanics in an interview setting.

**Key Discussion Points:**

**(0:50) The Current US Fiscal Situation:**
- "Through my roughly 50 years of being a global macro investor, I would keep to myself. And now I'm 75, and I want to pass along the things that have helped me."

**(6:23) The Big Debt Cycle:**
- "Only 20% of the 750 currency debt markets that have existed since 1700 still remain. And all of them that still remain have devalued."
- Big debt cycles typically last ~80 years
- The mechanics of unsustainable debt accumulation — "when debt service exceeds income"

**(24:54) USD vs Other Currencies:**
- Best hedges against dollar decline
- Gold, other hard currencies, diversification
- The currency question is the most misunderstood aspect of investing

**(33:20) Portfolio Construction:**
- Diversification across geographies and asset classes
- How China changes the risk calculation
- Why most investors have too much US exposure

**Notable Stats from Friedberg's Intro:**
- US federal government debt: $36.4 trillion (up 80% since pandemic)
- GDP: $29.1 trillion
- Debt-to-GDP: 125%
- Annual deficit: ~$2 trillion (nearly 7% of GDP)
- Annual interest on existing debt: >$1 trillion

**Key Quote:** "I want to make sure that's understood. How do the mechanics work for countries, for the United States, for other reserve countries?"

**Key Topics Covered:**

- Growing up in Jackson Heights, Queens
- Caddying at golf courses, overhearing Wall Street conversations
- Buying Northeast Airlines at age 12, tripling his money

**(30:00-1:00:00) Bridgewater Founding:**
- Working at Shearson Hayden Stone, punching his boss (fired)

- "Pain + Reflection = Progress" was born from this failure

**(1:00:00-1:30:00) Radical Transparency:**
- Why Bridgewater records every meeting
- How to create a culture where disagreement is welcome
- The biggest obstacle: the human ego
- "The two biggest barriers to good decision-making are your ego and your blind spots"

**(1:30:00-2:00:00) Meditation:**
- Dalio has practiced Transcendental Meditation for 50+ years
- How meditation helps him see the big picture
- "Meditation has been the single biggest factor in my success"

**(2:00:00-2:30:00) China:**
- His deep engagement with China over 30+ years
- Why he believes China will continue to rise
- The US-China relationship through the Big Cycle
- His advice for both countries

**(2:30:00-3:00:00) AI and the Future:**
- How AI will transform investing
- The importance of systematic decision-making
- Why AI can't replace understanding of cause-effect relationships

**(3:00:00-3:23:00) Final Thoughts:**
- His legacy — passing along principles, not wealth
- The Giving Pledge and philanthropy

## Evolution of Dalio's Public Voice

| Era | Tone | Focus | Key Themes |
|-----|------|-------|------------|
| **2007-2016** | Reclusive analyst | Economic machine | Sharing his Bridgewater framework privately |
| **2017-2019** | Teacher | Principles | "Let me teach you how I think" |
| **2020-2021** | Predictive analyst | Big cycle + debt | The pandemic as an accelerator of cycles |
| **2022-2024** | Concerned advisor | Paradigm shift | "The golden era is over; adapt or suffer" |
| **2025-2026** | Urgent warner | Stage 5 | "I'm not optimistic; prepare for radical change" |

The shift from 2024 to 2025-2026 is the most dramatic: Dalio went from "concerned educator" to "urgent warner." His language became more direct, his warnings more specific, and his assessment more pessimistic.

**2026 Quotes that Capture the Shift:**
- "The monetary order is breaking down" (CNBC, Jan 2026)
- "We are on the brink of capital war" (Dubai, Feb 2026)
- "It is indisputably clear that what is happening now is more analogous to pre-1945 times" (Fortune, Mar 2026)
- "Human nature being what it is, I'm not optimistic" (Fortune, Mar 2026)
