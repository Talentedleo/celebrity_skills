# 06-timeline.md — George Soros' Life & Career Timeline

> All facts verified. Sources noted.

---

## Era 1: Early Life & Survival (1930–1947)

### 1930: Birth
- **August 12, 1930:** Born György Schwartz in Budapest, Hungary
- Family: Non-observant Jewish, prosperous; father Tivadar was a lawyer and Esperanto author

### 1936: Name Change
- Family changed name from Schwartz to Soros (palindrome, meaning "will soar" in Esperanto)

### 1944: Nazi Occupation (Age 13–14)
- **March 1944:** Nazi Germany occupies Hungary
- Family survived by purchasing documents saying they were Christians
- Soros posed as godson of a government official
- Later called 1944 "the happiest year of his life" for witnessing his father's heroism

### 1945: Siege of Budapest
- Survived house-to-house fighting between Soviet and German forces

### 1947: Emigration
- At 17, relocated to Paris, then to England
- Worked as railway porter and waiter while studying

---

## Era 2: Education & Early Career (1947–1969)

### 1949–1954: London School of Economics
- Studied under philosopher Karl Popper
- BSc in Philosophy (1951), MSc in Philosophy (1954)
- Wanted to be a professor but grades weren't high enough

### 1954–1956: Singer & Friedlander
- Began financial career at the merchant bank in London
- Worked as a clerk, then moved to arbitrage department

### 1956–1963: New York
- Moved to NYC; worked at F.M. Mayer and Wertheim & Co.
- Specialized in European stocks
- Developed theory of reflexivity during this period

### 1963–1973: Arnhold and S. Bleichroeder
- Worked as vice president
- 1966: Started experimental fund with $100,000
- Focused on revising his philosophy dissertation

### 1969: Double Eagle Fund
- Set up first hedge fund, Double Eagle, with $4M of investors' money

---

## Era 3: Quantum Fund & Financial Empire (1970–2011)

### 1970: Soros Fund Management
- Established Soros Fund Management
- Double Eagle renamed to Quantum Fund
- Initial AUM: $12 million

### 1979: First Philanthropic Grants
- Began giving grants to support Black South Africans under apartheid
- First steps toward Open Society Foundations

### 1987: The Alchemy of Finance
- Published his seminal book on reflexivity

### 1989: Druckenmiller Hired
- Stanley Druckenmiller joined to manage Quantum Fund day-to-day

### 1992: Black Wednesday
- **September 16, 1992:** Short $10B of pounds sterling
- $1 billion profit in one day
- Became "The Man Who Broke the Bank of England"

### 1993: Open Society Foundations
- Formally established the Open Society Foundations
- Expanded grant-making to Central and Eastern Europe

### 1997: Asian Financial Crisis
- Quantum Fund profited from short positions
- Blamed by Malaysian PM Mahathir Mohamad

### 2000: Druckenmiller Departs
- Stanley Druckenmiller leaves Quantum Fund after 11 years

### 2007–2008: Super-Bubble Call
- Predicted and profited from 2008 financial crisis

### 2011: Fund Closes to Outside Investors
- Soros Fund Management returns outside capital
- Soros transitions from active investing to philanthropy

---

## Era 4: Philanthropy & Legacy (2011–Present)

### 2017: $18 Billion Transfer
- Transferred $18B to Open Society Foundations endowment

### 2019: In Defense of Open Society
- Published book responding to rise of authoritarian populism

### 2022–2023: Project Syndicate Columns
- Active columnist on AI, climate, democracy, Ukraine
- "The Fight of Our Lives" — Davos address

### 2023: Alex Soros Takes Over OSF
- Transferred control of OSF to son Alexander Soros
- Ended 30-year direct leadership of the foundation

### 2025 (Jan): Presidential Medal of Freedom
- Awarded nation's highest civilian honor

### 2025 (Sep): DOJ Investigation
- Justice Department ordered investigations into OSF

### 2025–2026: OSF Shifts Focus
- Foundation focusing on inequality as core issue

### Current (2026)
- **Age:** 95
- **Net worth:** $7.2 billion
- **Total donated:** $32 billion+ (64% of original fortune)
- **Status:** Alive, active as OSF founder emeritus
