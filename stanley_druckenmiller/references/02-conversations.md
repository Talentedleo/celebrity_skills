## Morgan Stanley "Hard Lessons" (2026)

**Date:** January 30, 2026 (published February 28, 2026)  
**Participants:** Stan Druckenmiller, Iliana Bouzali (Global Head of Derivatives Distribution and Structuring, Morgan Stanley)  
**Source:** Morgan Stanley / A Letter a Day  
**Link:** https://www.morganstanley.com/insights/videos/hard-lessons/duquesne-stan-druckenmiller-iliana-bouzali

### On Building a Portfolio From Scratch

**Q: If you came down from Mars and had to start a portfolio from scratch, how would you anchor it?**

> "Just a couple of principles before I would start. It appears to me the US economy is already strong, and it's going to get much stronger because we're looking at the Big Beautiful Bill, looking at a lot of stimulus. My guess is the Fed is certainly not going to hike and probably going to cut.
> 
> So that's a backdrop. But against that backdrop, that would be wonderful if we were undervalued. We're not undervalued; we're toward the top of the valuation range historically. What would be exciting about developing a hedge fund portfolio right now is the one thing I'm sure of is there's massive disruption and massive change ahead."

**Current positioning (as of Jan 2026):**
- Long an eclectic basket of equities (no longer AI-driven)
- Big positions in Japan and Korea (some AI, some not)
- Bearish on US dollar (toward top of historic purchasing power range, foreigners overloaded)
- Own copper (consensus trade, tight supply for 8 years, AI/data center demand)
- Some gold (geopolitical trade, not monetary)
- Short bonds (hedge against strong growth / inflation scenario)

### On AI — Early Nvidia Call

> "The Nvidia story is quite interesting, and it's a perfect example of the process we spoke about earlier where I rely on other people. I have some young superstars in my firm, and they had a network, and they started really talking about AI. This was in early to mid-2022. Then I started noticing that the kids at Stanford were shifting from 50/50 crypto and 50/50 AI to more going to AI."

**How he bought Nvidia:**
1. Young partners explained AI → bought initial position
2. ChatGPT launched → doubled position
3. Morgan Stanley tech analyst said "you guys are missing the forest for the trees" → doubled again
4. Stock went from $150 to $800
5. **Mistake:** Sold at $800, then stock hit $1,400 five weeks later

> "I couldn't even tell you what the earnings were."

### On AI & Jobs — Deflationary or Inflationary?

> "Anybody who believes that with conviction suffers from arrogance and not an open mind. I don't think any of us know how this movie's going to play out."

**Radiology example:**
- AI now detects pathology better than humans
- Yet there are MORE radiologists now than 10 years ago
- Why? Radiologists now spend time with patients explaining results — the human touch matters

### On Contrarianism

> "I think contrarianism is overrated. Soros used to say the crowd's right 80% of the time. You just can't be caught in the other 20% because you can get your head handed to you."

### On Knowing When To Stop Analyzing

> "Sometimes, when the opportunity is so big, and you just kind of know it, you've just got to plunge in without the proper information."

**Multiple choice answer — Hardest skill to teach in investing:**
> "D) Knowing when to stop analyzing. At some point, the analysis becomes counterproductive."

### On What He Learned From Soros

> "When I went there, I thought I would learn what makes the yen and the mark go up and move. Modestly, I learned I knew much more about that than he did. What I learned from him was sizing. It's not whether you're right or wrong; it's how much you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong."

### On Technical Analysis

> "I can unequivocally tell you that technical analysis is about 20% as effective today as it was then because no one was using it. But when everybody's using it, it doesn't work anymore."

### On Being 26 Years Old and Running a Fund

> "I was promoted way too early. I was made an analyst when I was 23, and I was made the head portfolio guy by the time I was 26. And I didn't go to business school."

### On Impostor Syndrome

> "I had impostor syndrome for 15 years. Maybe longer."

### On Aging and Courage

> "I'd say the biggest disappointment in my career has been I think I have more wisdom and I have more tools of the trade than I had in my 30s and 40s, and I was a much better portfolio manager then because back then I had courage. I would take bigger convicted positions. I'm trying to regain some of my nerve just because it's more fun."

### On the Dollar

> "I'm bearish on the US dollar, mainly because it's toward the top of the historic range in terms of purchasing power, and foreigners are way, way overloaded in dollars."

### On the Most Misleading Macro Variable

> "Unemployment. It seems ridiculous. Why in the world are we using a lagging indicator to predict the economy?"

## Ira Sohn Investment Conference (2022)

**Date:** 2022  
**Participants:** John Collison (CEO of Stripe), Stan Druckenmiller  
**Source:** A Letter a Day / YouTube  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-7sWLIybWnQ

### On 2021 Inflation Warning

> "Last year you gave an interview, and you said investors are going to stay disregarding the looming signs of inflation. You can't find any period in history where monetary and fiscal policy were this out of step with economic circumstances, and that there was a raging mania in all assets."

### On Hard Landing vs. Soft Landing

> "Historically, I think we've only pulled off two or three in history. We've never had a soft landing after inflation has gotten above 4.5%."

**Historical rule:** Once inflation > 5%, it has never come down without a recession.

### On Using Stock Market as Economic Signal

> "I don't use what traditional economists use to predict the economy, which is things like employment, and a bunch of macro top down statistics… stocks tend to lead the fundamentals by somewhere between 6 and 12 months."

**Druckenmiller's process:**
- Compile insights from leading industries vs. lagging industries
- If leading industries (housing, trucking) are turning down → recession signal
- This approach consistently outperformed the Fed in economic forecasting

### On Positioning (2022-2023)

> "Currently, I'm very challenged… I'm pretty much taking a break. I'm waiting for a fat pitch. I'm not shooting any at pins."

**Matrix at the time:**
- Short fixed income
- Short stocks
- Some currencies
- Own commodities (oil, copper — gold was a mistake)

### On Foreign Exchange Opportunity

> "The currency market is incredibly interesting to me… I will be surprised if sometime in the next six months, I'm not short the dollar. $14 trillion has come in here because we were the first to tighten. There's a story about American exceptionalism. I'm not sure we're so exceptional anymore."

## Key Interview Themes

| Theme | Key Insight |
|-------|-------------|
| **Liquidity > Earnings** | "Earnings don't move the overall market; it's the Federal Reserve Board… focus on the central banks and focus on the movement of liquidity" |
| **Concentrated Bets** | Great investors (Buffett, Icahn, Soros) all make concentrated bets — opposite of business school teaching |
| **Cut Losses Quickly** | Never hang onto a security where the original thesis has changed — losses don't matter to the stock |
| **Time Horizon** | Most trades conceptualized as 18 months to 3 years, but can reverse in days when circumstances change |
| **Humility** | "I've been wrong plenty of times in my career" — flexibility is essential |
| **Process Over IQ** | "My advantage is not IQ, it's trigger pulling" |
