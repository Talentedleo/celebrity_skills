# 04-External-Views

> How the world sees Michael Burry — media coverage, peer views, and public perception.

## Media Characterization

### Common Themes in Coverage
- "The investor who saw it coming" — the Big Short narrative
- "Cassandra" — the prophet who warns but isn't believed
- "Physician-turned-investor" — unusual background
- "Concentrated, contrarian bets" — high-conviction, concentrated positions
- "Quiet giant" — minimal public appearances, maximum impact

## Peer Assessments

### Warren Buffett
> Described Burry and John Paulson as "Cassandras" — investors who predicted the housing crash correctly but were dismissed.

### Elon Musk
> Called Burry a "broken clock" in 2021 after Burry correctly identified risks in Tesla.

### Alex Karp (Palantir CEO)
> Called Burry's $1.1B put options on Palantir and Nvidia "batshit crazy."

### Bill Ackman
> Burry agreed with Ackman's bullish call on Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac in March 2026.

### Joel Greenblatt / Vanguard / White Mountains
> Early investors who noticed Burry's message board track record before he started his fund.

## Controversies / Criticisms

1. **2008 investor revolt** — Some Scion investors wanted out during 2005-2007; Burry was vindicated but relationships were strained
2. **2021 "broken clock"** — Called Tesla and crypto a bubble; some said he was too early or wrong about specifics
3. **Palantir feud** — Called Karp's claims into question; Scion exited puts before drop, then closed fund
4. **Quiet periods** — No public communication for years at a time frustrates followers
