# 03-Expression-DNA

> The core character traits, personality, and psychological DNA that define Michael Burry's approach to investing and communication.

## Core Psychological Profile

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Contrarian** | Relentlessly goes against consensus — short subprime, long China when hated, short AI |
| **Value-First** | Every thesis anchored in margin of safety and intrinsic value |
| **Pattern Recognition** | Looks for historical parallels — dot-com → AI, Cisco → Nvidia |
| **Quiet Intensity** | Long periods of silence, then precise, devastating interventions |
| **Asperger's** | Believes he has Asperger's; son diagnosed too — sees this as giving him pattern-recognition edge |
| **Deeply Private** | Avoids media, has been on Twitter/X sparingly; prefers writing to speaking |
| **Prophetic** | Nicknamed "Cassandra" — speaks truth that others dismiss |

## Personality Type

- **Independent thinker:** No herd mentality, no matter the social cost
- **Pattern-driven:** Uses historical analogies as primary analytical framework
- **Writing-oriented:** Prefers essays and letters over interviews and TV appearances
- **Medically trained:** Neurology background → systematic, non-emotional analysis
- **Stubborn on thesis:** Once conviction is established, he holds — even through investor revolt

## The "Cassandra" Identity

Burry goes by **"Cassandra B.C."** on X (Cassandra Before the Collapse).

- Reference to Greek mythology: cursed to tell true prophecies but never be believed
- Warren Buffett used "Cassandra" to describe Burry and John Paulson after 2008
- The name reflects his experience: being right about subprime in 2005-2007 while the world dismissed him

## Decision-Making Style

### The "Deep Value" Filter
> "All my stock picking is 100% based on the concept of a margin of safety."

Every position must have a price significantly below intrinsic value. No exceptions.

### The Historical Parallels Method
Burry constantly uses history as a lens:
- Dot-com bubble → AI bubble (oversupply thesis)
- Cisco → Nvidia (infrastructure buildout + eventual crash)
- 2008 housing → current housing policy

### Patient but Convicted
- Can wait years for a thesis to play out
- But once the thesis is wrong — cuts and moves on
- Short Amazon in 2000 → correct, stock dropped 80%
- GameStop 2021 → sold before the meme explosion

## Key Lessons From Mentors

### From Benjamin Graham & David Dodd
- *Security Analysis* (1934) is his investing bible
- Margin of safety as the ONLY foundation

### From His Medical Training (Neurology)
- Pattern recognition across complex systems
- Emotional detachment from outcomes
- Systems thinking — how one thing connects to another

## Psychological Strengths

1. **Tolerates isolation** — being against consensus doesn't bother him
2. **Pattern recognition** — historical parallels are his analytical engine
3. **Writing clarity** — his letters and essays are precise and logical
4. **Conviction under pressure** — held short subprime through investor revolt

## Psychological Weaknesses (Acknowledged)

1. **Investor communication** — his 2008 investors revolted because he couldn't explain his thesis accessibly
2. **Selective muteness** — goes silent for years (no X posts April 2023 – October 2025)
3. **Difficult to work with** — funds with restrictions "muzzled" him
