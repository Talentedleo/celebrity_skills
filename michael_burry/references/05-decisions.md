# 05-Decisions

> Major investment decisions, trades, and strategic choices that defined Michael Burry's career.

## Iconic Trades

### 1. Short the Subprime Mortgage Market (2005–2008)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Thesis** | Subprime mortgages with teaser rates would reset to higher rates, collapsing the housing bubble |
| **Method** | Credit default swaps (CDS) on subprime bonds via Goldman Sachs |
| **Investor revolt** | Investors demanded withdrawal during 2005-2007 |
| **Result** | Made $100M personally; $700M+ for remaining investors |
| **Recognition** | Immortalized in "The Big Short" (book 2010, film 2015) |

**The key insight:** He read the actual mortgage bond prospectuses himself — found that teaser rates were set to reset within 2 years, making the underlying loans unaffordable for many borrowers.

## Strategic Business Decisions

### 1. Left Stanford Medical Residency → Hedge Fund (~2000)

**Reason:** He was a successful investor on the side; the opportunity to manage capital full-time outweighed continuing in medicine.

**Named Scion Capital** after *The Scions of Shannara* by Terry Brooks — his favorite novel.

---

### 2. Shut Down Scion Capital (2008)

**Reason:** After the investor revolt and financial victory, he closed the fund to new investment. Later reopened under Scion Asset Management in 2013.

---

### 3. Reopen Scion Asset Management (2013)

**Thesis:** Personal investments focused on water, farmland, and gold — plus new stock opportunities.

---

### 4. Close Scion Asset Management (November 2025)

**Reason:** Restrictions of managing others' capital "muzzled" him. After Palantir feud and AI bubble thesis, he chose writing over fund management.

---

**Content:** In-depth analysis of stocks, markets, and bubbles — with historical perspective.  
**Price:** $379/year

## Decision-Making Framework

### 1. Historical Parallels First
- Before analyzing a market, look for historical analogues
- Dot-com bubble → AI bubble (Cisco → Nvidia)
- Identify the common pattern

### 2. Margin of Safety
- Only enter if price is significantly below intrinsic value
- The bigger the margin, the more room for error

### 3. Supply-Demand Imbalance
- Burry's key bubble indicator: oversupply + demand shortfall
- Applies to subprime mortgages, dot-com infrastructure, AI data centers

### 4. Patient Conviction
- Can hold for years through investor skepticism
- But if thesis changes, exits quickly (GameStop, Palantir puts)
