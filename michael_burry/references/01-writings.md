## Investment Philosophy: Margin of Safety

**Core Foundation:** Burry's entire approach is built on one principle:

> "All my stock picking is 100% based on the concept of a margin of safety."
> — Michael Burry, multiple sources

**Source:** Benjamin Graham & David Dodd, *Security Analysis* (1934) — the textbook Burry returns to constantly.

**What this means in practice:**
- Only buy when price is significantly below intrinsic value
- Margin of safety absorbs calculation errors, bad luck, or market irrationality
- Burry: "The purpose of the margin of safety is to render unnecessary an accurate estimate of the future."
- Unlike classic Graham, Burry applies it to growth stocks too — but only when the price discount is extreme

### First Two Posts

#### Post 1: "Foundations: My 1999 (and part of 2000)"
Recounts his time as a neurology resident at Stanford, writing about value investing at night. Parallels his current return to writing after 25 years of hedge fund management.

#### Post 2: "The Cardinal Sign of a Bubble: Supply-Side Gluttony"

**Core thesis — AI is the new dot-com bubble:**

> "A key issue with the dot-com bubble was catastrophically overbuilt supply and nowhere near enough demand. And once again there is a Cisco at the center of it all, with the picks and shovels for all and the expansive vision to go with it. Its name is Nvidia."

**The "Five Horsemen" of today's AI boom:**
- Microsoft, Google (Alphabet), Meta, Amazon, Oracle
- Plus "several adolescent startups" including OpenAI

**Key argument:** 25 years ago, the Nasdaq was driven by "highly profitable large caps" including Microsoft, Intel, Dell, Cisco — yet the bubble still burst because of oversupply.

**Current AI capex:** Big tech has pledged to invest over $1 trillion in AI infrastructure (chips, data centers) over the next few years.

**His short position:** Burry's Scion held bearish put options on Nvidia and Palantir with a combined notional value of **$1.1 billion** as of September 30, 2025.

**Palantir feud:**
- Palantir CEO Alex Karp called Burry's puts "batshit crazy"
- Burry responded: "Karp couldn't crack a 13F"
- Scion exited Palantir puts in October 2025 before the stock dropped 26%

## Selected Quotes & Principles

### On Value Investing

> "The purpose of the margin of safety is to render unnecessary an accurate estimate of the future."

> "I don't think anyone should buy individual stocks without doing the deep fundamental analysis. And that analysis should be anchored in margin of safety."

### On Bubbles

> "If you go around popping a lot of balloons, you are not going to be the most popular fellow in the room." — Quoted Charlie Munger approvingly

> "Supply-side gluttony" — his term for the cardinal sign of a bubble

### On Being Contrarian

> "The market regularly overreacts to both good and bad news. This creates mispricing — opportunities for those willing to be against consensus."

### On His Medical Background

> Burry has noted that his neurology training affects how he thinks about markets — pattern recognition, understanding systems, and not being swayed by emotion.

### On Short Selling

> "Being short a bubble is like being long a value stock — you need patience, and the market can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent."

### On the Big Short Trade

From the NY Times op-ed: He believed by 2003-2004 that subprime mortgages and their bonds would begin losing value when original "teaser" rates reset to higher rates — often within 2 years of initiation.
