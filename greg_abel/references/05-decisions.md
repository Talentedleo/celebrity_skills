# Greg Abel - Key Decisions

*(Last updated: 2026-04-10)*

## Overview

Unlike Warren Buffett, whose investment decisions are well-documented through decades of shareholder letters, Greg Abel's decision-making is less visible because:
1. Most of his decisions relate to operational management of BHE, not public equity investments
2. He rarely discusses specific decisions publicly
3. The BYD investment was made by MidAmerican/BHE but involved Berkshire oversight

This document focuses on the decisions where Abel's role can be identified.

---

## BYD Investment (2008)

### The Decision

**Date:** September 2008  
**Deal:** MidAmerican Energy Holdings invested ~$230 million for 9.89% stake in BYD at HK$8 per share  
**Current Status:** Berkshire exited completely by September 2025

### Background

- **Who Made the Call:** The investment was made by MidAmerican Energy Holdings (BHE's predecessor), but Charlie Munger was credited by Buffett as the one who "saw the potential in the company"
- **Context:** Made during the 2008 financial crisis — countercyclical investment
- **Abel's Role:** As CEO of MidAmerican/BHE, Abel oversaw the subsidiary that made this investment

### What We Know About the Investment Thesis

1. **Munger's Vision:** Munger identified BYD's vertically integrated model (batteries + autos) as distinctive
2. **Early EV Bet:** In 2008, BYD was just beginning to enter automotive; the F3 DM plug-in hybrid launched that year
3. **Cost Advantage:** BYD's manufacturing cost advantage in batteries was evident

### Results

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Initial Investment | ~$230 million (September 2008) |
| Peak Stake | 9.89% |
| Berkshire Exit | 2022-2025 (gradual reduction) |
| Final Exit | September 2025 |
| Peak Value | ~$5-6 billion (estimated) |
| Total Return | Very significant (20x+ peak) |

### Lessons from BYD

- **Long-term thinking:** Berkshire held for ~17 years despite volatility
- **Munger's influence:** Shows how Munger's insights drove major decisions
- **Timing:** Entered during crisis, exited during strength

## Berkshire Hathaway Energy - Operational Decisions

### BHE Growth Strategy

Under Abel's leadership, BHE transformed from a regional utility into a diversified energy conglomerate:

**Key Acquisitions & Expansions:**

| Year | Action | Significance |
|------|--------|--------------|
| 1999 | Berkshire acquires controlling interest in MidAmerican | Entry point for Abel |
| 2000s | Expansion into wind, solar, geothermal | Diversification beyond coal |
| 2014 | Renamed Berkshire Hathaway Energy | Brand alignment with Berkshire |
| Ongoing | Regulatory strategy in multiple states | Stable cash flows |

### Operational Philosophy

Based on available information, BHE under Abel follows principles similar to Buffett's:

1. **Long-term infrastructure focus** — Regulated utilities with stable returns
2. **Decentralized management** — Local managers run their businesses
3. **Minimal interference** — "Let managers manage" philosophy
4. **Reinvestment discipline** — Cash reinvested in infrastructure vs. returned

## Leadership Decisions

### 2018 Vice Chairman Appointment

**The Decision:** Buffett elevates both Abel and Ajit Jain to Vice Chairman roles

**Abel's Side:**
- Given responsibility for all non-insurance operations
- Gained visibility into all Berkshire subsidiaries
- Mentorship from Buffett intensifies

**Outcome:** Viewed as critical step in succession process

### 2022 Share Purchase

**The Decision:** Abel purchased $68 million of Berkshire Class A shares (168 shares)

**Significance:**
- Showed personal confidence in Berkshire
- Aligns his wealth with shareholders
- Buffett had said he wanted successors who were "already very rich"

## What We Don't Know

Abel is notably private about his specific decision-making frameworks. Unlike Buffett's detailed explanations in shareholder letters, Abel has rarely articulated:

- His specific criteria for evaluating businesses
- How he thinks about capital allocation
- His views on specific industries or companies
- His investment decision-making process

**Most information comes from Buffett's public statements about Abel, not from Abel directly.**
