# Greg Abel - External Views & Criticisms

*(Last updated: 2026-04-10)*

## Overview

Greg Abel has received nearly universal praise from Berkshire insiders, but external analysts have raised some legitimate questions. This document captures both the positive views and the constructive criticism.

---

## Berkshire Insider Praise

### Warren Buffett

**2018 - On appointing Abel as Vice Chairman:**
> "They both have Berkshire in their blood. They love the company. They love the businesses. They know their operations like the back of their hand."

**2024 - On capital allocation capability:**
> "I would leave the capital allocation to Greg and he understands businesses extremely well. If you understand businesses, you'll understand common stocks."

**2025 - On future performance:**
> "The decision to keep every share is an economic decision, because I think the prospects of Berkshire will be better under Greg's management than mine."

### Charlie Munger

**2015 Berkshire Letter:**
> "Both are proven performers who would probably be under-described as 'world-class.' In some important ways, each is a better business executive than Buffett."

**2023 CNBC Interview:**
> "Greg is just sensational at being a business leader, both as a thinker and as a doer."

### Walter Scott Jr. (Berkshire Board Member, deceased 2021)

> "Greg is the kind of person that, whenever he's been asked to do something, he's willing to go and do it."

### Subsidiary CEOs

**Troy Bader, Dairy Queen:**
> "With Greg it's been wonderful. He's been very involved. He's a very accomplished individual. Very bright. Has a great understanding already of our business and so many of the industries."

**Brad Kinstler, See's Candies:**
> "At this point there is really no difference (between Buffett and Abel)."

## Analyst Perspectives

### JPMorgan (Sarah DeWitt)

**September 2023 Note:**
> "The most likely successor in our view, who Warren Buffett regularly praises, is Greg Abel."

**Stance:** Overweight rating; any selling pressure from Buffett's departure would be a buying opportunity.

### Key Analyst Concerns

While not publishing negative reports, analysts have raised these questions:

| Concern | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **No Independent Investment Track Record** | Abel has never publicly explained his investment decision-making |
| **Operational vs. Capital Allocation** | BHE is regulated utilities; very different from equity investing |
| **Succession Timing** | Some questioned if Buffett waited too long |
| **Privacy** | Limited public communication makes gauging his thinking difficult |

## Common Comparisons

### To Warren Buffett

**Similarities cited:**
- Work ethic
- Understanding of businesses
- Commitment to Berkshire culture
- Long-term orientation
- "Let managers manage" philosophy

**Differences noted:**
- Lessverbose, fewer stories
- More operational focus
- Less public profile
- No documented investment framework publicly articulated

### To Ajit Jain

Both named vice chairmen in 2018:
- Jain runs insurance operations
- Abel runs non-insurance operations
- Both praised by Buffett and Munger
- Munger said each is "better business executive than Buffett" in some ways

## Criticisms & Concerns

### 1. Lack of Public Communication

**Concern:** Unlike Buffett, Abel doesn't articulate his philosophy publicly.

**Implication:** Shareholders and analysts cannot gauge his thinking on key decisions.

**Mitigation:** Buffett's 60-year track record and stated confidence provides reassurance.

### 2. No Independent Investment Record

**Concern:** BHE's investments have been made under Berkshire oversight. Abel hasn't made standalone investment decisions at Berkshire's scale.

**Implication:** Unknown how he would handle $1 trillion+ in equity investments.

**Context:** Buffett explicitly said Abel would handle capital allocation, suggesting confidence.

### 3. Succession Uncertainty

**Concern:** Some questioned whether the succession was decided too late.

**Timeline:** Buffett confirmed Abel in 2021; actual CEO change in 2026.
- Five-year transition
- Some view as too long; others as prudent

### 4. BYD Exit Timing

**Concern:** Berkshire exited BYD gradually 2022-2025, missing some of the peak.

**Perspective:** Munger was the BYD champion; unclear if Abel shared that conviction.

## Balanced Assessment

### Strengths Recognized Externally

1. **Proven operator** — Successfully ran BHE for 15+ years
2. **Culture keeper** — Understood and preserved Berkshire's decentralized model
3. **Work ethic** — Known for engagement and preparation
4. **Trusted by Buffett** — Most important endorsement possible

### Concerns Acknowledged

1. **Limited public philosophy** — Hard to assess independent thinking
2. **No direct investment record** — BHE's regulated utility model differs from equity investing
3. **Communication style** — Less transparent than Buffett
4. **Scale question** — Managing $1T+ conglomerate vs. running utilities
