# Greg Abel - Interviews & Conversations

*(Last updated: 2026-04-10)*

## Overview

Greg Abel is notably private and rarely grants interviews. Unlike Warren Buffett, who is known for his accessibility and annual letter-writing, Abel has maintained a low public profile throughout his career. Most information about his views and management style comes from secondhand accounts, Berkshire official statements, and rare direct interviews.

---

## Rare Interviews

### FOX Business Interview (2018)

**Context:** First business network interview, conducted at Berkshire 5K run in Omaha

**Key Quotes:**
> "Nothing's changed. Business as usual. It's just great. We've got great people to be working with… couldn't be better."

> "Well, things are good right now. So our businesses are in a great place. And we've got great managers and like I said we are moving forward."

**Management Style Comments from Associates (reported in same piece):**

Troy Bader, President & CEO of International Dairy Queen:
> "With Greg it was wonderful. He's been very involved. He's a very accomplished individual. Very bright. Has a great understanding already of our business and so many of the industries."

Brad Kinstler, CEO of See's Candies:
> "At this point there is really no difference (between Buffett and Abel)."

---

### Horatio Alger Association Interview (2018)

**Context:** Abel's acceptance speech for Horatio Alger Award

**Childhood Stories:**
> "I remember getting together with other neighborhood kids as soon as we could after school. We played hockey nearly every day in summer and winter and stayed out until we were called in for dinner."

**On Making Money as a Kid:**
> "Growing up, Abel made money through odd jobs, like delivering advertising flyers and filling fire extinguishers for the company where his father worked, Levitt Safety."

**On Career Motivation:**
> "I want to have an impact. I want to roll up my sleeves and be actively engaged in making our company successful."

**On Work Ethic:**
> "I think hard work leads to good outcomes. In my schooling, in sports, and in my business positions, I learned that if I put in a lot of work and was well-prepared, then success would be more likely."

## Berkshire Annual Meeting Appearances

### 2025 Annual Meeting - First Address as CEO-Designate

**On Maintaining Berkshire's Culture:**
> "It's really the investment philosophy and how Warren and the team have allocated capital for the past 60 years. Really, it will not change. And it's the approach we'll take as we go forward."

**On His Role:**
> "I would maintain the reputation of Berkshire."

---

### Warren Buffett's Statements About Abel (Primary Source)

These represent the most direct "interviews" about Abel's capabilities:

**January 2018 - Vice Chairman Announcement:**
> "They both have Berkshire in their blood. They love the company. They love the businesses. They know their operations like the back of their hand."

**May 2024 - Annual Meeting:**
> "I would leave the capital allocation to Greg and he understands businesses extremely well. If you understand businesses, you'll understand common stocks."

**May 2025 - Succession Announcement:**
> "The decision to keep every share is an economic decision, because I think the prospects of Berkshire will be better under Greg's management than mine."

## Charlie Munger's Public Comments

**2015 Berkshire Shareholder Letter:**
> "Both are proven performers who would probably be under-described as 'world-class.' In some important ways, each is a better business executive than Buffett."

**2023 CNBC Interview:**
> "Greg is just sensational at being a business leader, both as a thinker and as a doer."

---

## Notable Absences

**Abel does NOT typically:**
- Grant media interviews (very rare exceptions)
- Write public letters or articles
- Speak at conferences outside Berkshire events
- Comment on specific investment decisions publicly

This privacy is in stark contrast to Buffett and makes research on Abel's specific views more challenging.
