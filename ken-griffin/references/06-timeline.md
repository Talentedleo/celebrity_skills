# 06-timeline.md — Ken Griffin's Life & Career Timeline

> All facts verified. Sources noted.

---

## Era 1: Early Life & Education (1968–1989)

### 1968: Birth
- **October 15, 1968:** Born in Daytona Beach, Florida
- Raised in Boca Raton, Florida
- Father: building supplies executive turned project manager at GE
- Grandmother: Genevieve Gratz — inherited oil business, farms, seed business

### ~1984–1986: High School
- President of math club at Boca Raton Community High School
- Ran EDCOM, a discount mail-order education software firm, out of his bedroom
- Predicted computer programming jobs would decline (in a 1986 article — while in a programming competition)

### 1986: Harvard College
- Fall 1986: Enrolled at Harvard College
- First investment: put options on Home Shopping Network — made $5,000 profit
- Installed satellite dish on Cabot House dormitory roof for real-time stock quotes
- Convinced Harvard to let him run the fund despite a ban on campus businesses

### 1987: Black Monday (Age 19)
- First formal fund launched with $265,000 from grandmother, dentist, and broker contact
- Entered with short positions built ahead of the crash
- Profited from October 19, 1987 Black Monday crash

### 1989: Harvard Graduation
- BA in Economics (with honors)
- Joined Frank Meyer at Glenwood Capital Investments in Chicago
- Meyer allocated $1M; Griffin returned 70% in one year
- Meyer advised: build a multi-strategy platform

---

## Era 2: Citadel Founding & Growth (1990–2007)

### 1990: Citadel Founded
- **December 1990:** Founded Wellington Financial Group with $4.6M (mostly from Meyer)
- Later renamed Citadel Investment Group (1994) and Citadel LLC (2013)

### 1991–1992: Early Returns
- 1991: +43%
- 1992: +40%

### 1994: Start of Multi-Strategy Expansion
- Expanded into statistical arbitrage
- Firm renamed Citadel Investment Group

### 1998: LTCM Crisis
- Restructured redemption terms (lockup or penalty)
- Weeks later, LTCM collapsed
- Financed: +30% for the year
- AUM grew from $2B to $6B by 2001

### 1999: Fixed Income & Macro
- Added fixed-income macro trading

### 2000: First Investment-Grade Rating
- Citadel became first hedge fund to receive investment-grade S&P rating
- Maintained it for 25 consecutive years

### 2001: Post-Enron Hiring Blitz
- Recruited Enron's energy talent the day after bankruptcy filing
- Launched commodities trading operation (2002)

### 2002: Citadel Securities Founded
- Market-making business established
- Initially low-profile, later one of the largest market makers in the U.S.

### 2003: Youngest on Forbes 400
- Age 34, net worth $650M
- Married Anne Dias

### 2006: Amaranth Acquisition
- Acquired energy portfolio at steep discount after Amaranth's $6B loss
- Issued $2B in public bonds (Lehman/Goldman) — second hedge fund ever to do so

### 2007: E-Trade & Sowood
- $2.5B investment in E-Trade (subprime portfolio + equity stake)
- 3:30 AM purchase of Sowood Capital's distressed assets
- Launched Citadel's first equity pod unit: Surveyor Capital (2008)

---

## Era 3: Crisis & Transformation (2008–2019)

### 2008: The Near-Death Experience
- Funds down 55%
- Levered 7:1
- Suspended redemptions (10 months)
- Griffin and partners injected $500M of own capital
- Testified before House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform

### 2009: The Rebound
- Funds returned +62%
- Awarded 2008: $900M personal earnings

### 2010: 20th Anniversary Letter
- Apologized for 2008 performance
- Shared four lessons learned
- Reflected on grandmother's early belief in Citadel

### 2012: High-Water Mark Reached
- Crossed 2008 high-water mark
- Began charging performance fees again

### 2013: Citadel LLC
- Citadel Investment Group renamed to Citadel LLC
- Sold E-Trade stake
- Divorced Anne Dias

### 2015: AUM Crosses $25B
- AUM reaches $25.5B
- Donated $40M to MoMA
- Donated $150M to Harvard for financial aid

### 2018: Ashler Capital
- Launched third equity pod unit: Ashler Capital
- Began returning excess profits to investors

### 2019: Capital Raising Halted
- Stopped actively raising new capital
- "We couldn't find enough opportunities to put additional money to work"

---

## Era 4: Dominance & Expansion (2020–Present)

### 2020: COVID Crisis
- Wellington Fund: +24.5% (S&P 500 fell 20% in Q1)
- Aggressive return to office — credited as edge
- Net worth surpassed $20B

### 2021: GameStop Controversy
- Citadel invested $2B in Melvin Capital
- Feb 18: Testified before House Financial Services Committee
- SEC: conspiracy theories unfounded
- Personal earnings: $1.8B

### 2022: Record Year — $16B Profit
- Largest single-year profit in hedge fund history
- Overtook Bridgewater as #1 all-time profitable fund
- Wellington Fund: +38%
- Decided to move HQ from Chicago to Miami

### 2023: Griffin Catalyst
- Launched Griffin Catalyst philanthropic platform
- $500M Harvard donation pledge
- Donated $2B+ total to charity
- Named Forbes Most Generous Givers

### 2024: Milken & CNBC
- Milken Institute Global Conference appearance
- CNBC Power Lunch interview: "The Fed is making the right choice"

### 2025: Continued Dominance
- AUM reaches $68B+
- Returns ~$5B to clients in distribution
- Bloomberg Talks podcast with half-hour interview
- CNBC education and economy interviews

### 2026: Wynwood Expansion & Davos
- January: $180M office building in Wynwood, Miami
- World Economic Forum in Davos (Bloomberg TV interview)
- Net worth: $51.2B (34th richest globally)
