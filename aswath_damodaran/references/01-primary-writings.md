# Aswath Damodaran - Primary Writings

## Overview

Aswath Damodaran is a Professor of Finance at NYU Stern School of Business, known as the "Dean of Valuation." He has authored multiple influential books on valuation and corporate finance.

---

## 1. Valuation Book Series

### "Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies" (Latest: 5th Edition, 2020)

**Publisher:** Wiley Finance

**Overview:** The definitive guide to valuation, covering tools and techniques for measuring and managing value.

**Key Sections:**
1. **Value and Valuation** - Understanding what value means
2. **Approaches to Valuation** - DCF, comparative, and contingent valuation
3. **Discounted Cash Flow Valuation** - Building DCF models
4. **Relative Valuation** - Using multiples and comparables
5. **Contingent Claim Valuation** - Real options and Black-Scholes
6. **Managing Value** - Linking valuation to corporate decisions

**Core Framework:**

> "The purpose of valuation is to estimate what an asset is worth, not to determine whether a stock is under or overvalued."

**Key Methodologies Covered:**

1. **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**
   - Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)
   - Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)
   - Terminal Value calculations

2. **Relative Valuation**
   - Comparable company analysis
   - Adjusted book value
   - Industry-specific multiples

3. **Contingent Valuation**
   - Real options
   - Decision trees
   - Monte Carlo simulation

**Source:** https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Valuation+Tools-p-9781119506598

**Key Posts:**

### "My Truth on Valuation: Myths and Mistakes" (2020)
**URL:** https://aswathdamodaran.blogspot.com/2020/04/my-truth-on-valuation-myths-and-mistakes.html

**Core Myths Debunked:**

1. **"Valuation is an exact science"**
   - Reality: Valuation is an art combined with numbers
   - Quality of inputs matters more than the model

2. **"A DCF is the only true valuation"**
   - Reality: All valuation models have strengths and weaknesses
   - Use multiple approaches for cross-checking

3. **"Quantitative models beat qualitative judgment"**
   - Reality: Numbers without context are meaningless
   - Qualitative factors can override quantitative analysis

4. **"Valuation should be objective"**
   - Reality: Valuations are always biased by the valuator
   - Acknowledge your biases

5. **"The market is always wrong"**
   - Reality: Markets may be wrong, but you may be more wrong
   - Don't assume market prices are always wrong

**Key Quote:**
> "The biggest mistake investors make is to confuse the simplicity of a valuation model with the complexity of the underlying business."
