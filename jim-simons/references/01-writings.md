## 1. Academic Mathematics Papers

### 1.1 Chern–Simons Form (1974)

**Co-author:** Shiing-Shen Chern
**Paper:** "Characteristic Forms and Geometric Invariants" (1974), Annals of Mathematics
**Source:** [Annals of Mathematics](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1971013)

The Chern–Simons form is a secondary characteristic class for 3-manifolds that emerged from work on the theory of characteristic classes. Originally a piece of pure differential geometry, it later became foundational in:

- **String theory** — providing a theoretical framework to combine geometry and topology with quantum field theory
- **Condensed matter physics** — describing topological phases of matter
- **Quantum field theory** — Albert Schwarz discovered early topological quantum field theory as an application of the Chern–Simons form

> "Chern–Simons theory is a gauge theory that describes the topology of 3-manifolds. It has had an enormous impact on modern theoretical physics." — Edward Witten

### 1.2 PhD Thesis (1962)

**Title:** "On the Transitivity of Holonomy Systems"
**Advisor:** Bertram Kostant, UC Berkeley
**Significance:** Gave a new proof of Berger's classification of the holonomy groups of Riemannian manifolds

**Source:** [DOI: 10.2307/1970273](https://doi.org/10.2307/1970273)

### 1.3 Simons' Formula & Simons Cone

- **Simons' formula:** A fundamental identity in minimal surface theory, relating the Laplacian of the second fundamental form to curvature terms
- **Simons cone:** A minimal cone that plays a key role in regularity theory for minimal surfaces

### 1.4 Oswald Veblen Prize (1976)

Awarded by the American Mathematical Society for Simons' geometric research contributions, particularly the Chern–Simons form and related work in differential geometry.

## 2. Renaissance Technologies & The Medallion Fund

### 2.1 The "Man Who Solved the Market" (2019)

**Author:** Gregory Zuckerman
**Source:** [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Man-Who-Solved-Market-Revolution/dp/073521798X)

The definitive biography of Jim Simons and Renaissance Technologies. Key insights:

**The Origin Story:**
> "Simons, the son of a shoe factory executive, was a brilliant mathematician who co-developed the Chern-Simons theory... In 1978, he left academia to start a hedge fund out of a Long Island strip mall next to a women's clothing boutique and a pizza restaurant."

**The Medallion Numbers (1988–2018):**
- Average gross annual return: **66.1%**
- Average net annual return (after fees): **39.1%**
- Total trading profits: **over $100 billion**
- Worst year: **+21%** net (2012)
- Zero losing years in 34 attempts

**Key Quote from Zuckerman:**
> "Simons proved that everything Wall Street believed about investing—the gut instincts, the narratives, the legendary stock-pickers—was theater. He showed that markets are solvable, that patterns exist in chaos, and that the most valuable edge isn't information or intuition—it's a better algorithm and the discipline to never override it."

### 2.3 The "Unified Model" Philosophy

From internal Renaissance documentation and employee accounts:

> "At Renaissance, everything feeds into one model. When someone improves the currency trading algorithm, it automatically benefits the equity algorithms because they're all interconnected."

**The architecture that made it work:**
1. **One model, not many** — All researchers work on a single shared codebase (several million lines)
2. **No information silos** — All code readable by every researcher
3. **Compensation tied to fund performance** — Not individual P&L. A researcher who improves someone else's signal is compensated the same

## 3. Simons Foundation Writings & Philanthropy

### 3.1 The Simons Foundation Mission

**Source:** [Simons Foundation](https://www.simonsfoundation.org)

Founded in 1994 by Jim and Marilyn Simons to support research in mathematics and fundamental sciences. Key programs:

- **Simons Observatory** — $50M+ project in Chile's Atacama Desert studying cosmic microwave background
- **Math for America** — Nonprofit founded 2004 to improve mathematics education in U.S. public schools
- **Simons Foundation Autism Research Initiative (SFARI)**
- **Flatiron Institute** — In-house computational research center

**2023 Record Donation:** $500 million to Stony Brook University — the second-largest donation to a U.S. public university in history.

### 3.2 "Eternal Sky" Film (2025)

**Source:** [Simons Foundation](https://www.simonsfoundation.org/2025/05/13/eternal-sky-film-about-jim-simons-and-the-simons-observatory-now-streaming/)

Documentary directed by Debra Kellner, chronicling the construction of the Simons Observatory. Key quote from the film:

> "I don't believe in destiny — I think we have to make our own destiny. I've done some mathematics, and I'm very proud of a number of things that I've done. But is it destiny? I don't think so. I think it's just perseverance you have to have. You have to believe in something and then just stick with it. Sometimes you realize this is just going nowhere, and you have to drop it. But I'm pretty tenacious, and that's worked for me." — Jim Simons

### 3.3 A Cosmic Merger Over Vegan Sprouts (2020)

**Source:** [Simons Foundation](https://www.simonsfoundation.org/2020/03/23/a-cosmic-merger-over-vegan-sprouts/)

Article by Brian Keating about the origin of the Simons Observatory. Key Simons quotes:

> "Why don't you and Adrian join forces with ACT and take out the competition?"

On cost of the observatory ($50M):
> "If I gave a dollar to each person who told me I'm a genius, even I'd be broke."

On the timeline for results:
> "Let's make it closer to 10 [years]."

## 4. Key Quotes & Philosophy

### 4.5 Return to Mathematical Research (Post-2010)

After stepping down from Renaissance, Simons returned to his first love:

> "I'm supposed to be retired. But I'm juggling a lot of things."

With Dennis Sullivan, he worked on differential characters (co-developed with Jeff Cheeger years earlier), now central to differential cohomology — his career coming full circle: mathematician → investor → philanthropist → mathematician.

### 4.1 On Investing & Markets

> "I don't want to have to worry about the market every minute. I want models that will make money while I sleep. A pure system without humans interfering."

> "We learned it's easier to teach smart people about markets than to teach market people how to be smart."

> "I'm not a stock picker. I'm a mathematician."

> "We're right 50.75% of the time, but we're 100% right 50.75% of the time. You can make billions that way." — Peter Brown (on the Medallion approach, channeling Simons' philosophy)

### 4.2 On Secrecy & Publicity

> "God gave me a tail to keep off the flies. But I'd rather have had no tail and no flies. That's kind of the way I feel about publicity." (quoting Benjamin the Donkey from Animal Farm)

### 4.3 On Perseverance

> "I think it's just perseverance you have to have. You have to believe in something and then just stick with it. Sometimes you realize this is just going nowhere, and you have to drop it. But I'm pretty tenacious, and that's worked for me."

### 4.4 On Talent & Culture

> "Hire the best possible mathematicians."

> "It's startling to see such a highly successful mathematician achieve success in another field." — Edward Witten
